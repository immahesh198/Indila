The Day Everything Changed
✦

June 11, 2025. I don't know if it was just another normal day for you, but for me it became a day I still remember clearly. I saw you at GMCC near the first-piece inspection table.

After that day, small things in my routine started changing. Sometimes I would arrive earlier than required on the shop floor or change shifts whenever possible, hoping that our paths might cross.

Then came Saturday, July 5th.

At that time I didn't know it was your last day in the company. I had heard from someone that you were leaving soon, but I didn't know exactly when. When you didn't come for the first shift, I assumed you had already left. That day I even changed my shift — if you remember. I think now you probably know the reason why.

Later, when you walked in for the general shift, I felt relieved. Honestly, I can't really describe how happy I felt in that moment. But I didn't realize then that it would be the last time I would see you there. Looking back, the only thing I truly regret is that I never simply came up and spoke to you.

A few months later, after moving to Kedgaon, I saw you again. You probably saw me too, if you remember — near a dosa stall on September 25th. I remember the date clearly. I chose not to come up and say hello because I didn't want to make things awkward or uncomfortable. I didn't know whether you would feel comfortable talking or not. Even now, whenever I see you, I honestly feel like talking to you. But for the same reason, I stayed quiet.

That moment stayed with me even after I left. I even called a friend afterward just to tell him that I had seen you again — the person who is most special to me. I just wanted to tell someone at that moment, that's why I called him.

I don't know when or how this message will reach you. Maybe someday, maybe not soon. But if it does, I just want you to know something honestly and simply.

You are someone I have always remembered with genuine respect and quiet admiration.

I don't know what you feel about me, or even if you feel anything at all. But I want to tell you honestly, Rutuja — you are the most special person to me in this world, and you always will be. Whether you feel the same way or not doesn't change that.

I don't know how you might feel reading this. Maybe you are surprised, maybe confused, or maybe even a little annoyed or mad. You seem like a slightly serious person — at least that's the impression I got. Of course, I might be completely wrong because I've never really had the chance to spend time with you. Maybe one day you can tell me whether I was right or wrong about that 🤞

I could never say all this face to face because I get nervous. That's why I chose this way to let this message reach you.

Of course, I took help from AI tools to build the code for this website because I'm not a professional coder. But still, putting everything together and making it work properly took a lot of effort. You can at least appreciate that part 🙂

And don't worry, this site is not public. Only you and I can see it.

Rutuja — you are the most special person to me.

What is NASA Artemis?
Artemis is NASA's human spaceflight program designed to return astronauts to the Moon for the first time since Apollo 17 in 1972 — over 50 years ago. Named after the twin sister of Apollo in Greek mythology, the program aims to land the first woman and the first person of colour on the lunar surface. Artemis is also the foundation for humanity's eventual journey to Mars.
Artemis II — The Mission
Artemis II is the first crewed mission of the program. Four astronauts will fly aboard NASA's Orion spacecraft, launched by the Space Launch System (SLS) — the most powerful rocket ever built. The crew will travel farther from Earth than any human has gone in over 50 years, looping around the Moon before returning safely. It is the critical test flight before astronauts land on the Moon again.
Send Your Name to the Moon
NASA invited people across the world to submit their names to be engraved on a microchip — a silicon chip no larger than a fingernail — that flies aboard the Orion spacecraft on Artemis II. Millions of names were etched onto this chip, which will travel 385,000 km to the Moon and back. It is a way for ordinary people everywhere to be part of one of the greatest journeys in human history.
Rutuja's Name — Aboard
Rutuja's name was submitted and officially certified as part of this mission. Her name is physically engraved on the chip flying on Artemis II. That means a small piece of her — her name, her identity — will travel to the Moon and back. Not many people can say that. It felt right to make sure she knew.
